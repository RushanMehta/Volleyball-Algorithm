Mathematical Formulation & Policy Derivation

This application treats a competitive volleyball set as a Single-Agent Markov Decision Process (MDP) with a Stationary Opponent Policy under standard Rally Scoring Rules.

1. The Bellman Optimality Equation

The solver calculates the globally optimized set win equity at every possible score state by selecting the action maximizing the downstream transition value:

$$V(u, t, \text{serve}) = \max_{a \in \{\text{TS}, \text{FL}\}} \sum_{u', t', s'} P(u', t', s' \mid u, t, \text{serve}, a) \cdot V(u', t', s')$$

Academic & Structural Disclosures
1. Intermediate Rally Aggregation Approximation:
Real match play propagates across complex multi-contact sequences (Serve $\rightarrow$ Pass $\rightarrow$ Set $\rightarrow$ Attack $\rightarrow$ Dig $\rightarrow$ Counter-attack). For computational tractability, this model applies an aggregated rally-win probability approximation, mapping downstream multi-contact transitions directly into localized defensive kill expectations based on initial pass quality metrics.
2. Stationary Strategy Limitation:
This model assumes a single-agent decision horizon against a fixed, stationary opponent policy profile (represented by a static sideout rate). It does not explicitly account for dynamic opponent adjustments, tactical variations, timeout pressures, or real-time substitutions.
3. Chain Truncation Bounds & Score Properties:
An exact absorbing Markov chain dealing with infinite deuce extensions contains infinite dimensions. This solver implements structural boundary truncation at a score cap, assuming independent event sequences with uniform transition states across all rotation intervals.
