Mathematical Formulation & Policy Derivation

This system models game propagation state tracking via Dynamic Programming over an Absorbing Markov Chain with a Stationary Opponent Policy under Rally Scoring Rules.

1. The Optimization Property Equation

The solver maps the optimized set win equity at every reachable coordinate position using backwards induction state reductions:

$$V(u, t, \text{serve}) = \max_{a \in \{\text{TS}, \text{FL}\}} \sum_{u', t', s'} P(u', t', s' \mid u, t, \text{serve}, a) \cdot V(u', t', s')$$

Explicit Boundary & Modeling Simplifications
1. First-Order Compressed Rally Model:
Real match play propagates across complex multi-contact sequences (Serve $\rightarrow$ Pass $\rightarrow$ Set $\rightarrow$ Attack $\rightarrow$ Dig $\rightarrow$ Counter-attack). For tractability, this system abstracts intermediate rally contacts into a single expected win probability based on initial passing metrics.
2. Stationary Strategy Assumption:
This model treats opponent defense matrices as constant vectors. It does not account for dynamic learning curves, non-stationary adjustment strategies, or defensive structural shifts.
3. Absolute Optimization Boundaries:
The outputs generated are calculated as "optimal solely under the specific structural assumptions of this absorbing Markov chain framework." Real-world tactical deviations may vary due to rotational asymmetries and non-independent point tracking.
